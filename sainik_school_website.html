Our Legacy

About Sainik Schools

India's premier residential institutions shaping the next generation of defence officers and national leaders since 1961.

🏛️ History & Origin

Sainik Schools were established by the Ministry of Defence in 1961 with the primary goal of preparing students for entry into the National Defence Academy (NDA) and the Naval Academy (NA). The first Sainik School was set up at Satara, Maharashtra — a pioneering institution that set the gold standard for all schools that followed.

🎯 Mission

To prepare students academically, physically, and mentally for careers in the Indian Armed Forces, while instilling values of discipline, integrity, and national pride that serve them in every walk of life.

🏫 Affiliation

All Sainik Schools are affiliated with the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE) and are managed under the Sainik Schools Society, an autonomous body under the Ministry of Defence, Government of India.

📍 Growth Across India

From a single school in 1961, the Sainik Schools network has grown to over 33 institutions spread across every region of India. Each school carries the same proud legacy, the same rigorous standards, and the same commitment to producing leaders who serve the nation with distinction. In 2021, the network was further expanded with new-pattern Sainik Schools set up in partnership with state governments and private partners.

⚖️ Governing Body

The Sainik Schools Society is the apex governing body, chaired by the Raksha Mantri (Defence Minister) of India. Each school is individually managed by a Board of Governors with representation from the state government, the Ministry of Defence, and the Army. Admissions are conducted centrally by the National Testing Agency (NTA) through the AISSEE exam.

Admission Guide

Admission Process

Admission is through the All India Sainik Schools Entrance Examination (AISSEE), conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) every year.

📝 Step-by-Step Admission Process

1

Check Eligibility

Verify age and class eligibility for Class VI or Class IX entry. Ensure your child meets the age bracket and has passed the prerequisite class from a recognised school.

2

Apply Online

Register on the official NTA / Sainik Schools Society portal during the application window (usually October–November). Fill the form carefully and upload all required documents.

3

Download Admit Card

Once registered, download the admit card from the official portal. This is required to appear for the AISSEE examination.

4

Appear for AISSEE

Sit for the written entrance exam conducted by NTA at centres across India, typically held in January. The exam covers Maths, Language, GK, and Intelligence.

5

Results & Shortlisting

Results are published on the NTA portal. Shortlisted students are called for the next stage based on merit and school-wise cut-offs.

6

Medical Examination

Shortlisted candidates must pass a medical fitness examination. Specific medical standards are published in the official AISSEE notification.

7

Final Merit & Admission

Final selection is based on written exam merit and medical fitness. Selected students receive an admission offer and complete document verification.

Class VI Entry

  • Age: 10–12 years as of 31 March of admission year
  • Must have passed Class V from a recognised school
  • Boys and girls eligible (varies by school)
  • Indian nationality required
  • Medical fitness is mandatory

Class IX Entry

  • Age: 13–15 years as of 31 March of admission year
  • Must have passed Class VIII from a recognised school
  • Subject to seat availability
  • Indian nationality required
  • Medical fitness is mandatory

Registrations open annually. Check the portal for current year dates and notifications.

AISSEE Preparation

How to Crack the AISSEE

The entrance exam is competitive but very achievable with the right strategy. Here is a complete subject-wise guide to help your child succeed.

📐 Mathematics

  • Arithmetic: percentages, ratios, fractions, decimals
  • Geometry: triangles, circles, angles, areas
  • Algebra basics: simple equations and expressions
  • Solve 30+ problems daily for speed and accuracy
  • Use NCERT books as the primary resource
  • Recommended: R.S. Aggarwal Mental Maths

🔤 Language (English & Hindi)

  • Build vocabulary with 10 new words daily
  • Practice reading comprehension passages regularly
  • Grammar: tenses, articles, prepositions, synonyms
  • Write short paragraphs to build composition skills
  • Read newspapers like The Hindu or Amar Ujala
  • Practise previous years' language papers

🌍 General Knowledge

  • Indian geography: rivers, states, capitals, mountains
  • Indian history: freedom movement, major events
  • Indian Constitution: fundamental rights and duties
  • Follow current events: national and sports news
  • Science basics: planets, human body, environment
  • Use Lucent's GK and monthly current affairs magazines

🧩 Intelligence & Reasoning

  • Series completion, analogies, and odd one out
  • Coding-decoding and direction sense questions
  • Pattern recognition and figure matrices
  • Venn diagrams and blood relation problems
  • Solve 20+ reasoning questions every day
  • Use R.S. Aggarwal Verbal & Non-Verbal Reasoning

🎯 Top 8 Exam Success Tips

1

Start preparation at least 6 months before the exam date for thorough coverage of all subjects.

2

Solve the last 5–7 years of AISSEE papers to understand the pattern, marking scheme, and difficulty level.

3

Daily schedule: 2 hrs Maths, 1 hr Language, 1 hr GK, 30 min Reasoning — every single day, no exceptions.

4

Take full-length mock tests every weekend under real exam conditions with a timer running throughout.

5

Focus on physical fitness too — the medical exam is equally important and requires good health standards.

6

Revise all topics in the final month. Do not start any new material 4 weeks before the examination.

7

Join a coaching class or online test series specifically designed for AISSEE for structured guidance.

8

Stay mentally strong. Consistent hard work, discipline, and a positive mindset always carry the day.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions every parent asks before applying to a Sainik School.

Are girls allowed in Sainik Schools? +
Yes! Since 2021, girls have been allowed to apply to Sainik Schools on a pilot basis. Several schools now admit girls, and this is being expanded across the network. Check the specific school's notification each year for eligibility.
What is the fee structure? +
Fees vary by school and state but are generally much lower than private boarding schools. Annual fees range from approximately ₹80,000 to ₹1,50,000 covering tuition, boarding, and meals. Scholarships and fee concessions are available for EWS, SC/ST categories.
Is the AISSEE exam very difficult? +
The AISSEE is competitive but very achievable. It tests Maths, Language, GK, and Reasoning at the Class V or Class VIII level. With 4–6 months of dedicated preparation using the right books and practice papers, a sincere student can absolutely crack the exam.
Is it mandatory to join the Army after Sainik School? +
No, it is not mandatory. While Sainik Schools primarily prepare students for the NDA, many alumni go on to IITs, IIMs, civil services, sports, entrepreneurship, and other fields. The education opens doors everywhere.
Can students visit home during the year? +
Sainik Schools are residential institutions. Students stay on campus but have scheduled leave periods during festivals, summer vacations, and other designated breaks to visit their families.
What medical fitness standards are required? +
Students must be in good general health with normal vision, no major physical defects, and a healthy weight range. A detailed medical examination is conducted after the written exam. Specific standards are published in the official AISSEE notification each year.
How do I choose which Sainik School to apply to? +
Many parents prefer schools in their home state for ease of visits and due to state-level reservations. However, you can apply to any school across India. Consider the school's NDA selection record, infrastructure, and proximity when making your choice.
When does the AISSEE registration open? +
Registration typically opens in October–November each year on the NTA portal. The examination is usually held in January. Always check the official NTA and Sainik Schools Society website for the current year's exact dates and schedule.
